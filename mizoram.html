Facts About

  1. Mizoram means 'Land of the Hill People

  2. Mizo Hills became a part of British India in 1895

  3. Majority of the tribes in Mizoram migrated to the state from neighbouring countries, dating back to 1500 AD

  4. Mizoram is one of the leading producers of bamboo in India, having about 27 species of bamboo

  5. The state bird of Mizoram is Mrs Hume's pheasant; the state animal is Capricornis Rubidus; and the state flower of Mizoram is the Renanthera Imschootiana

  6. Mizoram's first football league debuted in 2012 with eight teams

  7. Mizoram is the second least populous state in India

  8. Mizoram has many traditional dances. Some of them are Cheraw, Khuallam, Chheihlam, Chai etc.

  9. Mizoram holds the distinction of being the second most literate state in India, after Kerala

  10. Mizoram is one of the three states with 87 per cent of people practicing Christianity. It is believed that in the first half of 20th century, the tribes were converted from Animist religions to Christianity.

  11. Mizoram's first football league took place in 2012 with eight participating teams.

  12. Mizoram had to face mautam, a femine in 1959 due to excess rat population.

  13. Pukzing cave is the largest cave of mizoram. According to legends, it was carved just out of a hairpin by a man called Mualzavata.

  14. Milu Puk cave discovered some years ago.It was found to be full of heaps of human skeleton.

  15. In mizo culture, the groom must pay a certain price to a girl's family, to seek her in a matrimonial alliance.
