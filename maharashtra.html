Facts About Maharashtra

  1. The massive roadway

    Maharashtra has the largest road network in India with a total road length of over 2,67,500 kilometers.

  2. Have a swim in soda!

    It's the only saline soda lake on earth that was created when a meteor that hit earth almost 52000 years ago.

  3. Say no to doors!

    The houses in Shani Shingnapur have no doors. people believe that who ever tries to steal anything will face the wrath of god shani maharaj.

  4. The station of two states

    The Railway station of navapur is built in Two states.One half is in maharashtra and the other half is in gujarat.

  5. No food for the right reason!

    The dabbahwallah's of mumbai went on a strike for the first time in 2011 to attend and support Anna Hazare's anti corruption campaign.

  6. Massive per capita Income

    the per capita income of maharashtra state in 2014-15 was 134081 rupee.It was higher than the PCI of inida.

  7. Honesty is the best policy!

    Maharashtra has the largest proportion of taxpayers in india.

  8. Friendly cobras

    Each house in shetpal village has a resting place for live cobras in the rafters of thie ceilings, there has been no case of a cobra bite in that village till date.

  9. The hustle bustle of rush hour

    Mubmai locals were built to hold 1700 paseengers, but the often carry three times that number, in the rush hours.

  10. Home to many saints

    Some of the renowned saints of Maharashtra are shree swami samarth, shree sai baba and shree gajanan maharaj, tembe swami and narsing maharaj.

  11. The first Indian navy

    The concept of navy was introduced in india by shivaji maharaj. His admiral kanhoji angre is regarded as the father of india navy.

  12. Many gateways to Aurangabad

    Aurangabadf is known as the city of gates. a total 52 gates were built around this city in medieval era, of which only 13 survive today.

  13. The gold studded Nagpur

    Our country's entire gold assets are kept in the Reserve bank of india at nagpur.

  14. Inventor of Badminton

    It is believed that badminton was invented in Pune during the British rule. It was called as poonah or poona, after the town.

  15. First of Asia

    The first train in Asia was started in maharashtra between mumbai and thane on april 16, 1853.
